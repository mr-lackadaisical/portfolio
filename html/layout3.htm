Daisy

There is a little, dainty flower, That lifts its golden eye, Without a single tinge of shame, Unshrinking to the sky; But yet, so sweetly free from art, It captivates the thoughtful heart! It glads the merry month of May, On August smiles a cheer; It greets the pale October day, "The saddest of the year"— And still an open bosom shows Amid the cold December snow. It roams upon the mountain-top, To catch the morning sun; It plays about the meadows, where The merry brooklets run; Upon the forest solitudes The pretty daisy's form intrudes. And oft-times on the infant's grave, This little flower is found; Nor aught more fitting thus to bloom On consecrated ground; 'Tis beautiful without pretence— An emblem sweet of Innocence!
